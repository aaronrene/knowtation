## Situation (from research)

- **Umbrella first:** Born Free is how we communicate the **member-managed ecosystem**—places, experiences, Keys, credits, governance—not “storage SaaS” as the headline. **Store Free** is the document, signing, and provenance tool (standalone or inside the journey); keep that split explicit in external messaging.
- **Layered story:** When a page must mention documents, point to **Store Free** positioning and its instructional voice; do not collapse Born Free into storage slogans. (See competitive snapshot plus both Store Free and Born Free voice guides in `depends_on`.)
- **ICP / lead lanes:** Lead ICP for paid Keys / member growth: (1) People optimizing for recurring stays, predictable annual credits, and fair rotation of booking priority over years. (2) People who want collective stewardship, local creators and projects, and governance tied to place-based economics—not only personal nights.
- **Evidence discipline:** Strategy claims must cite `depends_on` paths or evidence recorded under `projects/store-free/research/public-sources-2026.md` (canonical URL registry for Born Free and Store Free app URLs). Use Knowtation product URLs and social handles only as listed in **`projects/knowtation/research/public-sources-2026.md`**. Competitive snapshot items may carry **[NEEDS CONFIRMATION]** until sourced.
- **Knowtation (sibling):** Use Knowtation in umbrella or ecosystem copy when the piece is explicitly **cross-product**; keep voice aligned with `projects/knowtation/style-guide/voice-and-boundaries.md` for product-specific pages (whitepaper §1.1 platform tools).
- **Competitive / matrix claims:** `projects/store-free/research/competitive-snapshot-2026-04-24.md` stresses layered Born Free vs Store Free messaging; **named competitors, pricing bands, and market sizing** stay **[NEEDS CONFIRMATION]** until backed by additional research or product sign-off—do not invent a competitor matrix from this outline alone.

## Strategic choices

1. **Primary ICP (umbrella)** — Anchor on what §1.1–1.3 of the whitepaper states: people who want collective paths to real property, community-aligned access, and legible on-chain rules for booking, credits, and governance—not generic “crypto investors.” The whitepaper contrasts that audience with traditional timeshares, high capital and operations barriers, illiquidity, and weak member governance. Born Free is a member-managed network for real-place experiences—Experience Keys tie stays, renewable credits, and community governance to on-chain rules you can read, not a hidden back office.
2. **Category frame** — Name the problem in membership, access, and governance terms, not storage. The whitepaper’s own frame: community-building network around collective property and Experience Keys as “Digital Keys to Real Experiences,” tying property access to DAO governance through on-chain rules. **Position as utility-driven access and governance** (membership), **not** fractional real-estate speculation or opaque “ownership” claims—Experience Keys deliver access and utility, **not direct property ownership** (§1.3).
3. **Proof we will lead with** — Restrict to shipped or documented-in-whitepaper facts—for example Avalanche deployment, contract and test counts, launch timestamp, and architecture stack as listed in §1.4—plus live URLs only after verification and only as written in `projects/store-free/research/public-sources-2026.md` (Born Free rows). Do not describe availability, financial outcomes, or booking results as guaranteed (voice guide §5). Do not treat internal funnel or unapproved docs (for example `bornfree-pwa/docs/TOP-FOLD-QUICK-REFERENCE.md`) as **compliance-approved** proof until product and legal align those steps and numbers with the live funnel.

## Message hierarchy

**External headline rule:** Lead with Keys, place, credits, and member governance; when the reader's job is documents, hand off to Store Free without making storage the umbrella headline.

- **Pillar 1** — Digital Keys to Real Experiences  
  Experience Keys bundle stay utility, rotating priority, and governance participation with rules on the ledger, not in a hidden admin layer.  
  **Headline guardrail:** Umbrella copy leads with **Keys, place, credits, governance**—not storage.  
  **Proof:** `bornfree-pwa/public/whitepaper.md` §1.1–1.3; Hub mirror: **`projects/born-free/research/whitepaper.md`** (see `projects/store-free/research/public-sources-2026.md`).

- **Pillar 2** — Fair, explicit access over the long cycle  
  8-year tier round-robin, June booking windows, annual credit replenishment (mechanics in §1.3 and §1.5).  
  **Proof:** same whitepaper sections; messaging guardrails in §1.5.9 (“ALL RR#s are valuable”).

- **Pillar 3** — Community stewardship of place  
  Collective purchase, local projects and creators, DAO proposals and voting; surplus from real-world stays, events, and on-property offerings—after operating costs and agreed reserves—can flow into community-aligned economics (treasury and governance outcomes), alongside credits and marketplace activity, as described in the whitepaper sections cited below.  
  **Proof:** `bornfree-pwa/public/whitepaper.md` §1.1; **`### 6.1 Revenue Streams` → `#### 3. Property Profits`**; marketplace context in §6.1 **2. Marketplace Fees** where relevant.

- **Pillar 4** — Culture and creative work, grounded in place  
  The network exists to **establish place and community**: collective stewardship of real property, local projects and creators, and experiences where culture and shared governance reinforce each other—not a disconnected “audience platform.”  
  **Proof:** `bornfree-pwa/public/whitepaper.md` §1.1; `projects/born-free/style-guide/voice-and-boundaries.md` §1 (who we help); optional economics hook: §6.1 **3. Property Profits**.

- **Pillar 5** — Join on your terms  
  Email-first onboarding; optional wallet and self-custody when members are ready; honest framing of on-chain costs where applicable—supports the umbrella story without making document custody the headline (hand off document detail to Store Free when needed).  
  **Proof:** §1.3 Shared Platform; §5.2 User Journey (including optional Store Free / Knowtation depth per step 7); §1.1 platform tools list; §6.1 **Strategic fit** (Store Free as gateway, without collapsing Born Free into storage).

## Forbidden claims

- Nothing here is approved for external use until checked against the whitepaper and live product.
- Born Free does **not** promise **investment returns**, **guaranteed booking** or other **guaranteed outcomes**, or **legal, tax, or medical** results—see `projects/born-free/style-guide/voice-and-boundaries.md` §5. For law, tax, or securities-sensitive topics, point readers to qualified professionals rather than improvising authority (same section).
- Do not invent **metrics**, **testimonials**, or **case study numbers**; cite whitepaper or registry paths, or label projections explicitly.
- No **competitor or individual trash talk**; compare **capabilities and tradeoffs** only.
- Avoid **hype vocabulary** the style guides call out (for example WAGMI, “moon,” guaranteed life-change copy).
- **Urgency:** only **real** dates, **real** capacity, **honest** deadlines (voice §6).
